If you take your coffee seriously, you've probably already dismissed the functional coffee category. And based on most of what's out there — instant powders, earthy flavours, wellness aesthetics dressed up as nutrition — that was a reasonable call. But the category has evolved. We tested it with a speciality coffee consultant on the panel to find out whether any of it is worth a serious coffee drinker's time.

☕ Why coffee purists dismissed this category — and why that's changing

The early wave of "mushroom coffee" products had a reputation problem that was entirely earned:

  • 😬Earthy, fungal taste — when extracts make up 30–40% of the product, you taste them
  • 🧪Instant coffee bases — freeze-dried, months old, nothing like freshly roasted
  • 🥄Powders and rituals — whisks, shakers, a whole new process
  • 📦Wellness packaging over coffee quality — clearly designed for the supplement aisle, not the coffee shelf

The result: anyone who knew what good coffee tasted like took one look and kept walking.

Real coffee drinkers don't want a supplement. They want their morning cup — upgraded. Not replaced. Not disguised. Not diluted.

The ritual is the point. Performance coffee should fit into it — not disrupt it.

☕ What we looked for in a coffee-first functional blend

  • 🫘Real ground coffee as the base — freshly roasted, not freeze-dried instant
  • ⚖️90%+ coffee ratio — extracts shouldn't dominate the formula or the flavour
  • 🌿Flavour-neutral extracts — fruiting body sources, not mycelium on grain
  • 📍Known sourcing — where is the coffee from, who grew it, when was it roasted
  • 🔬Meaningful functional dose — not a marketing claim, a real amount

90% freshly roasted Arabica. 10% functional extracts. The ratio that keeps the coffee tasting like coffee.

🍄 The 90/10 ratio — why it matters

The reason most functional coffees taste like functional coffees is the ratio. When extracts make up 20–40% of the blend, you taste them. The solution is simple: keep the coffee at 90% and make sure the 10% of extracts are flavour-neutral.

myco:soul uses fruiting body extracts — the part of the mushroom where the active compounds concentrate — rather than mycelium on grain, which is cheaper and more commonly used. Fruiting body extracts are both more potent and more flavour-neutral. This is the ingredient choice that makes the taste difference.
